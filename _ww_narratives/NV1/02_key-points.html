• Women across the world, regardless of income, age or education, are subjected to physical and sexual violence perpetrated by current or former intimate partners.
  • One in three women will experience physical and/or sexual violence by an intimate partner at some point in her life.
  • 18% of women and girls have experienced physical and/or sexual violence by a current or former intimate partner in the previous 12 months.
  • The prevalence of intimate partner violence in the previous 12 months varies widely between countries, from 2% to 46%, although some issues in data comparability remain.
  • As at 2020, at least 153 countries have passed laws on domestic violence.
  • Regional differences in the rates of intimate partner violence also persist, with women in Oceania (excluding Australia and New Zealand), Southern Asia and sub-Saharan Africa regions at greatest risk.
