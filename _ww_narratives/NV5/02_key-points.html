• According to the United Nations Children’s Fund (UNICEF) and the World Health Organization (WHO), at least 200 million adolescent girls and women have undergone FGM based on recent data from 31 countries.1,2
  • FGM is slowly declining in selected countries and subregions where the practice is more prevalent.
  • Despite recent progress, the prevalence of FGM remains alarmingly high in parts of Northern Africa and West Africa.3
  • Because COVID-19 is interrupting programmes to end FGM, progress may be threatened.
  • Progress in the elimination of FGM is not universal, and where it is taking place it is not fast enough.4 Even in countries where the practice has become less common, progress would need to be at least 10 times faster to meet the global target of its elimination by 2030.
    • Based on the latest available data, in six countries5 at least 3 out of every 4 women and adolescent girls aged 15–19 have undergone FGM.
