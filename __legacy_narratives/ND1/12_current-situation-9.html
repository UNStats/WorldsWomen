This argument is also supported by the historical data on the number of female CEOs on the list of the Fortune 500,11 which shows the largest corporations in the United States of America. While the list from 2020 has more female CEOs than ever before, women only account for 7.4% of the total number: only 37 out of 500 CEOs are women (see figure VII).

While this shows an improvement from 1998, when only 1 out of the top 500 corporations had a female CEO, the gender gap remains significant and is even more pronounced than among large enterprises, where in 2018 the share of women CEOs was reported at 16% (figure VII).
