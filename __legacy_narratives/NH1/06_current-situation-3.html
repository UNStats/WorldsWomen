In 1998, younger mothers were more likely than older mothers to deliver their babies with the assistance of skilled health personnel, while by 2014 little difference was recorded by age. Data collected in 1998 show a marked difference in the proportion of births attended by skilled health personnel according to a woman’s age, a difference that has faded over time as more women have gained access to professional health care. By 2014, among women aged 15–49, the proportion of women giving birth with skilled medical assistance was above 70%, and the difference among age groups was negligible compared to previous years (see table 1).
